## What I teach

**Electrical engineering fundamentals** — breadboards, circuits, wiring, generators, motors, sensors, and embedded systems. Students get hands on the hardware; the theory anchors what they've already touched.

**Robotics** — VEX IQ team coaching and VEX V5 mentorship. Mechanical design, motor and sensor integration, autonomous programming, testing, and competition strategy.

**Programming** — Python, Java, C++, Lua, plus Roblox Studio and Minecraft modding for the younger cohort. The languages are different; the ideas — control flow, data, feedback — are the same.

**AI and vision** — VEX AI vision-sensor applications for object detection and robot positioning.

## How the coaching runs

Students bring their build to the bench. I ask them to explain what they did before I say anything. Then we debug together. The point is to grow their ability to reason about the system — motors, sensors, code, feedback — not to fix their robot for them.

## What the numbers look like

More than one hundred elementary and middle-school students taught to date, across programming, robotics, electrical engineering, and AI-vision units.
