problem: |
  Score student robotics teams fairly and give them feedback specific enough to actually improve their next build.
process: |
  Structured interviews with each team using the official rubric, plus notes on things the rubric can't quite capture (design intent, iteration evidence, teamwork under pressure).
challenges: |
  Balancing consistency with individuality — every team explains itself differently, but the rubric has to apply the same way.
solution: |
  Ask the same core questions in the same order, then leave room for the follow-ups that reveal how the team thinks.
lessons: |
  Judging teaches you what a design brief looks like from the outside. Half of what makes an engineering answer good is being able to say it out loud.

Judging VEX IQ competitions across Texas — interviewing teams, applying the official rubric, and writing feedback the students can actually act on. The best teams don't necessarily have the fanciest robot; they can explain why every choice on the robot exists.
